Camp Information

Basic Camp Information

This is a FREE camp for any female students in the San Diego area who start grades 8-12 in the fall of 2016. The camp is sponsored by the Women in Computer Science club at Westview High School and is taught by current Computer Science major college students.

Goal

It is our goal to introduce girls to the world of computer science and teach them the basics of programming through the use of guest speakers from a variety of fields and fun, interative programming projects. After learning the basics, we hope they have the tools they need to continue their programming education through self-teaching or other programs.

When and Where

The camp is being held August 8th- August 12th 2016 at Westview High School (13500 Camino Del Sur, San Diego, CA 92129). There will be 2 different classes taught as listed below. Please note this is a slightly different set-up than last year and that it is NOT recommended for students who have already taken AP Computer Science to sign up for the camp this year.

Time Class Level Class Level Description Programming Language(s) Learned
9:00am-12:00pm Beginner This class is for students who have never seen programming before. The class will start by working with Scratch and talking about how to think about code. By the end of the beginner course,students will learn up to loops and if statements. Scratch, Java
12:30pm-3:30pm Intermediate This is a class for students who have either taken the beginner course at this camp last year, or have some experience programming. In the intermediate course, students start with a review of topics from data types to loops. By the end of the course students will have gone up to data structures such as stacks and queues. Students who have taken AP Computer Science will be too advanced for this class. Java

If you have any questions about which class to sign up for, please contact us at sdgirlscodingcamp@gmail.com
